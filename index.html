1.1 What is Dyslexia?

Dyslexia is a condition that makes it very difficult for children to read, write and/or spell. It has nothing to do with the person’s intelligence. Dyslexia describes a different kind of mind, sometimes gifted and productive, that learns differently.

It is a specific learning difference that is neurological in origin. It can go undetected in the early years of schooling. Children may become frustrated, show signs of depression, and low self-esteem, and develop a dislike for school.

About 10% of Singaporeans may be dyslexic. Dyslexia is life-long but can be overcome with specialist teaching and compensatory strategies.

What causes Dyslexia?

The exact cause is uncertain, but research suggests neurological differences in the brain that may be inherited. These differences affect how dyslexics think, learn, and process information.

  • Phonological processing difficulties
  • Poor short-term memory
  • Trouble learning additional languages

Effective teaching tailored to a child’s learning style can help remediate difficulties. Language structure and exposure also affect the severity of symptoms.

1.2 Signs and Symptoms

Early Signs

  • Difficulty remembering nursery rhymes
  • Pronouncing sounds
  • Learning sound-letter connections
  • Remembering words seen many times
  • Remembering instructions
  • Uncertainty of left or right

At Primary School Level

  • Difficulty learning alphabet, numbers, days of week
  • Spelling and writing name
  • Retelling a story in sequence
  • Slow to add new vocabulary
  • Cannot recall the right word

Other Common Signs

  • Poor fine motor control and hand-writing
  • Difficulty copying, attention problems
  • Issues with punctuation, organization of ideas
  • Confuses symbols, sequences, concepts of time

1.3 Screening Checklist

Dyslexia Screening Booklet is a referral tool for teachers and parents to help identify literacy difficulties in primary school children.

Using the Booklet

  1. Complete all sections
  2. Parents to fill in checklist
  3. Attach recent samples of student work
  4. Submit to school’s Educational Psychologist

Teacher's Checklist

Reading

  • Reads very slowly or hesitantly
  • Loses place or skips lines
  • Difficulty recognizing familiar words
  • Guesses or misreads words frequently

Phonology

  • Difficulty identifying beginning sounds
  • Difficulty identifying rhyming words

Spelling/Writing

  • Poor spelling and grammatical structure
  • Messy or inconsistent handwriting
  • Bizarre spelling (e.g., 'pokegt' for 'pocket')
  • Wrong order of letters (e.g., 'hlep' for 'help')

Motor Skills

  • Appears clumsy
  • Difficulty catching/throwing or skipping

Speech, Concepts and Memory

  • Persistent word searching
  • Difficulty with left and right
  • Difficulty telling time or remembering days

Behaviour

  • Difficulty staying on task or in seat
  • Easily distracted or frustrated
  • Lacks interest/motivation
  • Constantly seeks attention

Physiology

  • Problems with eyesight or hearing?
